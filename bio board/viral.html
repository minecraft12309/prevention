Viral Diseases

Viral diseases are caused by viruses, which are microscopic pathogens that can infect living organisms, including humans, animals, and plants. Unlike bacteria, viruses cannot reproduce on their own; they require a host cell to replicate.

AIDS

  • Causative Organism: Human Immunodeficiency Virus (HIV)

  • Transmission: Blood, sexual contact, sharing needles, and from mother to child during childbirth or breastfeeding.

  • Symptoms: Early symptoms may include flu-like symptoms (fever, fatigue, swollen lymph nodes); chronic infection can lead to AIDS (Acquired Immunodeficiency Syndrome), characterized by opportunistic infections and certain cancers.

  • Prevention: Safe sex practices, pre-exposure prophylaxis (PrEP), avoiding sharing needles, and regular testing.

  • Treatment: Antiretroviral therapy (ART) to manage HIV infection and improve immune function.

Hepatitis

  • Causative Organism: Various viruses (Hepatitis A virus (HAV), Hepatitis B virus (HBV), Hepatitis C virus (HCV), Hepatitis D virus (HDV), Hepatitis E virus (HEV))

  • Transmission: Contaminated food and water, blood, sexual contact, mother to child.

  • Symptoms: Fatigue, jaundice (yellowing of the skin and eyes), abdominal pain, nausea, and loss of appetite.

  • Prevention: Vaccination for HAV and HBV, safe food and water practices, and avoiding sharing needles.

  • Treatment: Supportive care for HAV and HEV; antiviral medications for HBV and HCV.

Dengue Fever

  • Causative Organism: Dengue virus

  • Transmission: Aedes mosquitoes.

  • Symptoms: High fever, severe headache, pain behind the eyes, joint and muscle pain, rash, and mild bleeding.

  • Prevention: Mosquito control measures, use of insect repellent, and avoiding standing water.

  • Supportive care; acetaminophen for pain and fever; avoid non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs) like aspirin.
